## Introduction

In this project, you will design and implement a simple personal finance manager application using classic object-oriented design patterns in Python. The goal is to help users track income and expenses, monitor their balance, and receive alerts when the balance falls below a certain threshold.

## Design Patterns

### Applying Foundational Patterns

You will use foundational design patterns from the **creational**, **structural**, and **behavioral** categories to implement various components of the application.

These include:

- **Singleton Pattern** – to manage the balance.
- **Adapter Pattern** – to convert third-party transactions into an app-compatible format.
- **Observer Pattern** – to notify users of critical events, such as low balance and balance changes.

### Refactoring for Modularity

You will also refactor your code to support **dependency injection**. This will increase the modularity of your application and make it easier to test.

### Testing Your Components

You will write **unit tests** to validate component behavior. These tests will demonstrate how design patterns improve the maintainability of your code.

### Final Deliverable

Your final deliverable will be a **Python application** organized in a **modular structure**, accompanied by:

- Unit tests.
- Documentation explaining how each design pattern was used and why it was appropriate for the given scenario.
